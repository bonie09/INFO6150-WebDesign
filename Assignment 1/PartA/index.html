Recommended destinations


Mumbai

India's commercial powerhouse and one of its most important port cities is without a doubt Mumbai. Bollywood, the hub of the influential Hindi film and television industries, is located in Mumbai, making it the most diverse and cosmopolitan metropolis in all of India. The greatest slum population in India is also located there.

Bangalore

The capital of the Indian state of Karnataka is Bangalore, which is often referred to as Bengaluru. It is the third-largest city in India, with an estimated 8,749,944 residents (Urban 2011). Bangalore is the 12th cleanest city in India. Bangalore, also referred to as the Silicon Valley of India, is the main hub of the Indian IT sector.

Delhi

The capital territory of India, Delhi, is a massive metropolitan area in the country's north. The imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard can accommodate 25,000 people, are both located in Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating back to the 1600s.

Hyderabad

The state of Telangana is located in southern India, and its capital is Hyderabad. It is a significant hub for the technology sector and has a large number of posh eateries and stores. Golconda Fort, formerly the capital of the Qutb Shahi dynasty and a major diamond trading hub, is one of its historic landmarks.
